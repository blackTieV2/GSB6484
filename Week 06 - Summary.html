Main Topics

BP Gulf of Mexico: Definitions

Social Legitimacy

Social Legitimacy: The acceptance and approval of an organization by society, achieved by aligning organizational values and practices with societal norms and expectations to gain and maintain public and stakeholder trust.

Reputation Management

Reputation Management: Actions taken by an organization to influence and control stakeholder perception, aimed at protecting and enhancing the organization’s image and brand.

Social & Environmental Disclosure

Social & Environmental Disclosure: The practice of providing information about an organization’s social and environmental impact, policies, and practices with the intention of informing stakeholders and demonstrating the organization’s commitment to CSR and sustainability.

BP Gulf of Mexico: BP’s Actions

What did BP do?

  • Extensive Public Reporting: BP provided detailed and frequent updates about their response efforts, environmental impact assessments, and corrective actions on their website and through press releases.
  • Corrective Action Plans: BP outlined and implemented corrective measures to address the oil spill, including stopping the leak, cleaning up the spill, and preventing future incidents.
  • Commitment to Stakeholder Compensation: BP committed to compensating affected stakeholders, including local communities, businesses, and individuals impacted by the spill.
  • Environmental Impact Assessments: BP conducted thorough environmental assessments to understand the extent of the damage and guide their remediation efforts.
  • Transparency & Accountability: BP emphasized transparency and accountability in their communications, providing detailed information about their actions and progress.

Why did BP do it?

  • Rebuild Trust & Credibility: BP aimed to rebuild trust and credibility with stakeholders by demonstrating their commitment to addressing the spill and its impacts.
  • Mitigate Negative Public Perception: BP sought to mitigate negative public perception by proactively communicating their response efforts and progress.
  • Regain Social Legitimacy: BP aimed to regain social legitimacy by aligning their actions with societal expectations and norms.
  • Legal & Regulatory Compliance: BP needed to comply with legal and regulatory requirements, including environmental laws and compensation mandates.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: BP engaged with stakeholders to address their concerns and demonstrate their commitment to remediation and compensation.
  • Long-term Reputation Management: BP focused on long-term reputation management to restore their image and ensure the sustainability of their operations.

Case Study: BP Gulf of Mexico Disaster

Case Study: BP Gulf of Mexico Disaster

The BP Gulf of Mexico oil spill, also known as the Deepwater Horizon spill, occurred on April 20, 2010, when an offshore drilling rig exploded, resulting in a massive oil spill. This incident is considered one of the worst environmental disasters in history, with severe impacts on marine and coastal ecosystems, local communities, and businesses dependent on the Gulf of Mexico.

BP's response to the disaster included extensive public reporting, implementation of corrective action plans, commitment to stakeholder compensation, and conducting environmental impact assessments. The company aimed to rebuild trust and credibility, mitigate negative public perception, regain social legitimacy, comply with legal and regulatory requirements, engage with stakeholders, and manage their long-term reputation.

Homework Questions and Detailed Answers

1. Discuss the importance of social legitimacy for a corporation, especially in the context of a crisis.

Social legitimacy is crucial for a corporation as it represents the acceptance and approval of an organization's actions by society. During a crisis, maintaining social legitimacy becomes even more critical as stakeholders closely scrutinize the corporation's response. In the context of the BP Gulf of Mexico disaster, BP needed to align its actions with societal norms and expectations to regain public trust and mitigate the reputational damage caused by the spill. By demonstrating transparency, accountability, and commitment to remediation, BP aimed to restore its social legitimacy and ensure the long-term sustainability of its operations.

2. Analyze BP’s reputation management strategies following the Gulf of Mexico disaster.

BP employed several reputation management strategies following the Gulf of Mexico disaster, including:

  • Extensive Public Reporting: BP provided frequent updates on their response efforts, environmental impact, and corrective actions through their website and press releases.
  • Corrective Action Plans: BP outlined and implemented measures to stop the leak, clean up the spill, and prevent future incidents, showcasing their commitment to addressing the crisis.
  • Stakeholder Compensation: BP committed to compensating affected stakeholders, demonstrating their responsibility towards those impacted by the spill.
  • Environmental Impact Assessments: BP conducted thorough assessments to understand the damage and guide their remediation efforts.
  • Transparency & Accountability: BP emphasized transparency and accountability, providing detailed information about their actions and progress.

These strategies were aimed at rebuilding trust and credibility, mitigating negative public perception, regaining social legitimacy, complying with legal and regulatory requirements, engaging with stakeholders, and managing their long-term reputation.

3. Explain the role of social and environmental disclosures in managing corporate reputation during a crisis.

Social and environmental disclosures play a critical role in managing corporate reputation during a crisis by providing transparency and demonstrating the organization's commitment to addressing the issue. These disclosures help inform stakeholders about the company's actions, policies, and practices, showcasing their efforts to mitigate the impact and prevent future incidents. In the case of BP, their extensive public reporting, corrective action plans, and environmental impact assessments were essential in managing their reputation and regaining public trust. By proactively communicating their response and progress, BP aimed to rebuild credibility and ensure the long-term sustainability of their operations.
